A quiet hour steals the afternoon,

The sun hangs low, a coin of faded gold. And all the scattered thoughts, like dust, Begin to settle in the silent % cold. I watch the shadows stretch and reach and thin, And see the years in patterns on the floor. A younger ghost plays games where I have been, And finds a memory behind each door. The clock's soft ticking is a gentle hand, That marks the passing,   not with sound or fuss,
But with a slow and patient-held command, To leave behind the former shape of us. And so I write, while autumn's colors burn,
A different leaf on each remembered year.
To hold the moment, watch the pages turn, And find the quiet poetry of here.
